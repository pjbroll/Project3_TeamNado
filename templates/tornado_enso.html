Tornado Prediction

o forecast tornadoes or not forecast tornadoes? That is the question!

But really, how can we predict something as seemingly random as a tornado! El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO) is a large scale feature that drives global weather patterns. ENSO is an irregularly periodic variation in winds and sea surface temperatures over the tropical eastern Pacific Ocean, affecting the climate of much of the tropics and subtropics. The warming phase of the sea temperature is known as El Niño and the cooling phase as La Niña.
In 2015 researches found a connection between ENSO and the amount of tornado activity in the U.S during the spring severe weather seasons (Allen, et al, 2015). We've used Linear Regression to see if such a predictive signature is present in the data. The data used is ENSO Area 4.3 Sea Surface Temperature (SST) anomaly data (difference from the seasonal average), and total tornado counts by month. The period of record is 1950-2018. If there is a relationship we can use forecasts of ENSO - like this 2020 forecast - to "predict" how many tornadoes we can expect during the spring months.
Now look at some of the results. Research says the signal is best during mid-Spring. April appears to show less Tornado activity during strong El Niños and more Tornadoes during La Niñas as "predicted."
